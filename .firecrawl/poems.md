# The Air You Breathe

And the curve of your smile

The fall of your hair

And the blush on your cheeks

The lines on your nose

And the chill on your spine

The gum of your teeth

And the dimple on your chin

The love in your heart

And the hope in your eyes

The beauty of your soul

And the kindness of your being

The way you walk

And the way you go blank

The way you think

And the way you sway

The joy in your life

And the boy in your life

The truth in your voice

And the trust in your love

They are all mine

And only mine

For the air you breathe

Is the air I breathe

When our lips are close

And our foreheads touch

When our eyes are closed

And our souls feel peace

Come be with me

For you no longer

Will know any worry

Of the world and all

In our bubble of love

Of peace and joy

Of pure and soft

Of you and I.

# Life

![Life](https://www.sarathmenonfilms.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2Fimg_5.6d8ab7af.jpg&w=1080&q=75)

Young Lola asked her grandfather,

“Baba! You’ve seen life, haven’t you? What’s it like?”

“It’s a lot of sleep and eating is all that is.”

Baba said with a giggle.

Lola went silent inside her mind.

So did Baba.

After a few breaths eased away, he asked,

“Why do you ask?”

“I thought you’ll be an expert by now since you’ve done life for so long.”

Lola wanted her answers.

“Then hear it.”

And he came out of his shell and spoke,

“Life is like a tortoise.

Slow yet moving forward,

Sometimes hard to notice,

When it hides in the shell,

Always beating itself down,

For lack of what it takes,

Running a race it didn’t ask for,

Believing to be failing,

Only when you crawl till the end,

One knows what a win it was,

To just be breathing.”

# Sunset

![Sunset](https://www.sarathmenonfilms.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2Fimg_6.67dc6540.jpg&w=1080&q=75)

The sun was setting

When I was told

That my grandfather

Breathed his last

Miles from home

I felt his presence

Beside my self and

In my heart

I ran and flew

All those miles

Between my self

And my first teacher

Everything I own

I owed to him

From the wit on me

To the genes in me

The sun was setting

In my mind

To think of a world

Without that kind soul

His gist with words

And wit of the world

Were like none else

And pure like gold

The moment I saw

The cold and stiff

Body once hosted

A gentle warm soul

I shed my tears

Out of love

All that love

I still had to give

The day went by

And so did he

The people left

And so did the noise

The stillness set

In the home he built

Where his kith and kin

Now live like kings

But the real King

In his mind

And his regal soul

Was the one we lost

Mornings come by

And so do moods

We live and laugh

And we eat and drink

But when the sunsets come

Life gets tough

For it brings back the thoughts

Of the one that set in our lives.

# Rain

![Rain](https://www.sarathmenonfilms.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2Fimg_8.042fc737.jpg&w=3840&q=75)

When I was woken up

From my deep sleep

I felt the chill and mood

Of an unwelcome rain

Pulling up the clothes

And struggling with my tie

I remember my eyes

Brimming to rain

The first hour of school

Dingy and gloomy

Felt quite looming

In the light of rain

The first bell rang

And we looked outside

At the puddles of water

That welcomed feet

Our feet stayed still

Under the cold desks

And we learned to spell

Count and speak

Years went by

I was woken up again

By the chill and mood

Of a similar rain

The clouds were full

And the sky was grey

The puddles came back

With no bells to be heard

Today the rain brings calm

Now it is welcome

And helps me feel

The soul of earth

The air feels fresh

When my skin gets wet

As my feet no longer

Want to stay indoors

My hair first felt

The love of rain

As I jumped onwards

From puddle to puddle

The men looked struck

And the women in awe

Street vendors smiled

As I became a child

Today the rain is welcome

But not too much

As the strays feel cold

And the land fears flood

But the rain is rain

As it always has been

Like a curse or a blessing

That we prefer or not

The leaves didn’t mind

Neither did the roots

Or the shallow rivers

Of the motherland

Why do we hide

From the laws of nature

When we belong

Within that mother?

# Always

![Always](https://www.sarathmenonfilms.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2Fimg_9.bfcc1391.jpg&w=1080&q=75)

Since when have we known each other?

Days?

Weeks?

Or have we always?

They say when two people meet, their lives will never be the same again.

Like when you read a book, you are never the same person again by the time you finish.

Some books, we love.

Some others, we don’t feel like reading.

Some others, we read and put down without completing.

And some other books stay with us. We want to read them again.

Every time adding a new perspective to our mind. Changing us. One page at a time.

We all are like books.

You and I too.

Since I came across a book with such a pretty cover, I decided to find out what is inside.

And inside I found such a lovely story filled with life.

A life so familiar, that I couldn’t stop turning pages.

Pages so rich in poetry, that I couldn’t help but wonder how the verses were crafted.

Craft so lovely, that it comes only second to the cover of the book itself.

The book so lovely, it has begun to change me.

The book tells me the story of everything that I once used to be.

Everything that I always wanted to be.

And everything that I still hold deep within my heart.

A story that I have always known to exist.

A story, that only I thought I knew existed.

When that story was told to me in the pages, I wondered, is this my story?

Then I realised, the story is neither mine, nor anyone else’s.

A story, a tale, is something you familiarise with.

Something that you find to be your own.

What is within you.

Since when have we known each other?

Days?

Weeks?

Or have we always?

# My friend Tony

![My friend Tony](https://www.sarathmenonfilms.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2Fimg_10.04d938bd.jpg&w=1080&q=75)

I watched my friend Tony

Count his precious money

To pay for lady Leone

And her deadly alimony

Tony’s pool was full

And his mind quite lull

Covered in soft wool

He had her pull

His roof was tall

And dry like fall

Now it leaks is all

Over the grand hall

They once were naïve

And had chosen to believe

In this life none deceive

A life they now leave

For my dear friend Tony

His heart is now stony

After all, his sweetest honey

Has gone with all his money

Her mind was sane

And she hoped in vain

The blood in his vein

Would never give her pain

The pool is now dry

But filled with his cry

Tony didn’t know why

She said her goodbye

Tony, learn your lesson

That a life of oppression

Must not be your profession

Is my humble suggestion
